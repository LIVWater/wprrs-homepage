Honorary
Home/About/Honorary Members

Service recognised.

Honorary membership is conferred on members whose contribution to refereeing in Western Province — across many years, often quietly — has set the standard for the rest of us.

These are the people who built the Society — on the field, on the committee, and in the years between.

Honorary membership is the highest recognition the Society can give a member. It marks decades of contribution to refereeing in Western Province — long service on the executive, sustained excellence on the field, mentorship of others, or all of these. Honorary status is granted by member vote at the AGM.

Process

How honorary status is conferred.

The award is rare by design. The process makes sure that honorary status remains a meaningful recognition of sustained contribution.

01

Nomination

Any active member may nominate another, with a written motivation describing the contribution being recognised.

02

Executive review

The executive reviews each nomination against the criteria set out in the By-Laws — sustained service, contribution to development, and standing in the Society.

03

Member vote

Approved nominations are put to the membership at the AGM. Honorary status is conferred by majority vote.

04

Recognition

Honorary members are recognised at the AGM and at the annual awards evening, and their names are added to the published roll.
