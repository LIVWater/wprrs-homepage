Constitution & by-laws.

The Society's foundational documents — defining who we are, how decisions are made, and the rights and obligations of every member.

The Constitution is the Society's foundational document. It sets out the purpose, structure, powers, and rights that frame everything we do. The By-Laws sit beneath it — operational rules covering elections, fees, meetings, appointments, and discipline. Both are reviewed periodically and amended by member vote at the Annual General Meeting.

The current versions are Issue 6, dated 28 October 2021. They are published in full and available for download below.

Download the current issues.

Both documents are open to members and the public. Print copies are available from the Referees' Office on request.

Inside the Constitution

What the document covers.

The Constitution is structured around the powers and protections that members need to know about. Each section is short, plainly worded, and numbered for reference.

01

Name, status & affiliation

Legal status of the Society, its affiliation with the Western Province Rugby Union and SA Rugby, and its operating area.

02

Objects

The purpose of the Society — recruiting, training, grading, and appointing referees for sanctioned games in Western Province.

03

Membership

Categories of membership — Active, Standby, Honorary — eligibility criteria, fees, and the process for application and resignation.

04

Executive Committee

Composition of the committee, the eight portfolios, term length, nomination, and election procedures.

05

Meetings

Notice and quorum requirements for the AGM, Special General Meetings, and committee meetings.

06

Finance & reporting

Banking, signing authorities, audit, financial reporting to members, and the budget approval process.

07

Amendments

The procedure for amending the Constitution — proposed amendments, notice periods, and the majority required.

08

Dissolution

Procedure in the event of dissolution and disposal of the Society's assets.
