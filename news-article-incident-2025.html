Incident 20 July 2025 Western Province Rugby Referees' Society

Physical assault of a match official.

A formal statement from the Western Province Rugby Referees' Society regarding an incident at the Manenberg Rangers vs. Albions fixture.

On Saturday, the Western Province Rugby Referees' Society became aware of a serious incident at the Manenberg Rangers vs. Albions fixture. A match official appointed by the Society was the subject of a physical assault during the course of the match.

The Society condemns this incident, and all forms of violence against match officials, in the strongest possible terms. There is no circumstance — competitive, emotional, or otherwise — in which the assault of a match official can be tolerated or excused.

Rugby cannot exist without referees. An assault on a match official is an assault on the game itself.

What we are doing

The Society's Match Reviewing and Discipline portfolios have opened a formal investigation. The affected member has been offered the full support of the Society, including welfare check-ins and access to professional support if required.

We are engaging with the following parties:

  • The South African Police Service, who have opened a case
  • The Western Province Rugby Union's disciplinary committee
  • The affected clubs and their executives
  • SA Rugby, in line with national reporting protocol

A formal disciplinary process will follow the SAPS investigation. Sanctions available to the Union and the Society include — but are not limited to — fines, individual playing bans, club-level suspensions, and forfeiture of fixtures.

Where we stand

The Society's position on the welfare of match officials is unchanged and non-negotiable. Every appointed referee is entitled to officiate a sanctioned fixture without fear of harm. Every club that fields a team has a duty of care to the match official appointed to their game.

We acknowledge that the overwhelming majority of fixtures across Western Province are played in the spirit in which the game was built. We acknowledge the work clubs do to manage their players and supporters. None of that changes the simple fact that one incident of this nature is one too many.

What we ask of clubs

To every club playing under WPRU affiliation, three things:

  1. Brief your players, coaches, technical staff, and supporters that any physical contact directed at a match official is a matter for the criminal justice system as well as the Union.
  2. Where any club official witnesses or is aware of behaviour escalating, intervene early and visibly.
  3. Submit incident reports promptly. The Society can only act on what is recorded.

What members can do

Members may also be approached by media, opposition supporters, or social media commentators in the days ahead. We ask that all members refrain from public commentary on the specifics of the incident and direct any enquiries to the Society's administration.

The Society will publish further updates as the investigation progresses. Members will be briefed directly through the next virtual meeting and the Chairperson's monthly update.

Issued by the Executive Committee, Western Province Rugby Referees' Society.
