Training & development.

A structured pathway from your first whistle through Division 1 and beyond — World Rugby certification, regional coaching, fitness, and player welfare at every level.

The pathway

One programme, four pillars.

Every active member moves through the same four-pillar development programme — calibrated to your grade, but built around the same principles.

Start your application Course calendar
01

World Rugby certification

The foundation is built online via the World Rugby Passport. Rugby Ready, Introduction to Rugby Refereeing, and Laws of the Game are mandatory for every active member, with Level 1 and Level 2 in-person courses delivered by the Society throughout the year.

Start World Rugby courses Apply to the Society
Foundation
02

Regional coaching sessions

Each region has a dedicated coach running weekly sessions during the playing season. The focus is positioning, communication, signal clarity, and law application — building the on-field craft that exams can't teach.

Find your regional coach Session calendar
Coaching
03

Fitness testing

Annual mandatory testing using the YOYO intermittent recovery protocol — YOYO 15 for entry-level, YOYO 17 for higher divisions. Fitness scores are one of the three grading inputs and a hard requirement for promotion.

Book your YOYO test See grading thresholds
Fitness
04

Match reviewing

Senior assessors attend matches across the Province and produce written reviews on positioning, decision-making, communication, and law application. Every active referee receives regular structured feedback as part of grading.

Grading System

From Beginner to Division 1 — and beyond.

The grading system is transparent and merit-based. Three measurable elements determine where you sit and when you move up: fitness test results, examination marks, and on-field performance reviews. Each division has its own criteria.

Match Appointments

Where you officiate.

Appointments are managed through WHISTLE — our digital platform — with notification by SMS and email. Your division and availability determine the games offered.

Club fixtures

Approximately 90 affiliated clubs across Western Province play 160+ sanctioned fixtures per weekend across senior, junior, and women's competitions.

School matches

Wherever capacity permits, we appoint referees to school matches across the Province — a key development opportunity for junior and beginner refs.

Provincial competitions

Higher-graded referees are appointed to provincial competitions including Currie Cup First Division, Varsity Cup, and SA Schools rugby.

BokSmart Safety

Player welfare is non-negotiable.

The BokSmart National Rugby Safety Programme is a joint venture between SA Rugby and the Chris Burger/Petro Jackson Players' Fund, designed to reduce catastrophic injuries through education, certification, and best-practice protocols on contact.

01
Mandatory accreditation

No coach, referee, or support official may enter the playing enclosure without a valid BokSmart accreditation. Recertification is required every two years.

02
Course delivery

WPRRS hosts accreditation courses multiple times each season. The course covers concussion management, scrum safety, and the Blue Card protocol.

03
MyBokSmart Online

The 6th instalment of the BokSmart Rugby Safety Course is now fully online via the MyBokSmart platform. Sign up at my.boksmart.com to certify or recertify.

04
Blue Card protocol

A Blue Card removes a player from the field for suspected concussion and triggers the graduated return-to-play protocol. Every referee must know it and apply it.

05
Vision Zero

BokSmart's #VisionZero campaign targets the elimination of catastrophic spinal injuries from South African rugby. Referees are central to that goal.

06
Resources

Blue Card data, return-to-play guidance, and GRTS infographics are available through the Society. Contact the office for current dates and downloads.
