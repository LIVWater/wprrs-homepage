If it happened, it gets reported.

Every appointed match generates a report. Every serious incident generates a dedicated incident report. Both are submitted through WHISTLE on the same day.

If you do not record what happened, the chances are strong that the player, club, school, or spectator will do it again.

Match reports are the Society's institutional memory. The match report records the basic facts. The incident report triggers Society action — disciplinary, judicial, or club-level — when behaviour crosses the line. Both protect players, fellow officials, and the integrity of every fixture across the Province.

Two forms

One per match — and one when it matters.

The match report is submitted for every appointment. The incident report is submitted in addition, whenever an event requires the Society to act.

Required · Every match

Match Report

Submitted by the appointed referee after every sanctioned fixture. Records the score, the cards issued, injuries reported, and any non-routine event. Filed through WHISTLE on match day.

Required · When triggered

Incident Report

Submitted in addition to the match report when an incident requires Society follow-up — physical assault, sustained verbal abuse, dangerous foul play, or any behaviour that brings the game into disrepute.

Match Report

What goes in the report.

The match report is short by design. The aim is to record the facts in a way the Society can scan in seconds and act on if needed.

01

Fixture & result

Competition, round, venue, kick-off time, home and away clubs, final score, and your name with grading at the time of appointment.

02

Cards

Yellow and red cards issued — number, time of issue, player name and number, club, offence under the Laws, and your sanction reasoning in one sentence.

03

Injuries

Injuries reported during the match — Blue Card cases, suspected concussion, any injury requiring medical removal. Note the time and the player's club.

04

Match notes

Anything non-routine — venue issues, behaviour from technical areas or spectators, weather impact, late kick-off, abandoned play. Brief and factual.

05

Submit

File through WHISTLE on match day. Late or missing reports affect your grading. If you cannot submit on the day, contact the Match Reviewing portfolio.

Incident Report

When to file an incident.

The incident form is for serious matters. If you are unsure whether to submit one, submit one — the executive will determine whether action is warranted.

Physical assault

Any physical contact directed at a match official before, during, or after a fixture. Whether or not the contact connected. Whether or not it was identified at the time.

Sustained verbal abuse

Persistent, targeted abuse from a player, coach, technical area, or spectator. Single comments handled in-match do not require a separate report. Sustained patterns do.

Dangerous foul play

Foul play requiring citing — red-card offences that may warrant additional sanction beyond the in-game decision. Include time, player number, and your description of the action.

Field invasion or game stoppage

Any spectator entering the field, game stopped or abandoned for crowd or behaviour reasons, or anything that brings the game or the Society into disrepute.

Match official welfare

An incident affecting the welfare of you or a fellow official — intimidation, harassment, threats. The Society treats officials' welfare with the same priority as players'.

Anything unclear

If you cannot decide whether an incident requires a separate report, file one. The Match Reviewing portfolio will assess and route appropriately. Better filed than missed.

Process

What happens next.

Reports are reviewed by the Match Reviewing portfolio holder, then routed through the relevant Society and Union channels.

01
Match day

Match report submitted through WHISTLE before midnight on the day of the fixture.

02
Within 48 hours

Match Reviewing portfolio reads every report. Incident reports are flagged and routed to the disciplinary process.

03
Within 7 days

Disciplinary process initiated where required. The reporting referee may be asked for additional written or in-person evidence.

04
Confidentiality

Reports are confidential to the Society and the relevant disciplinary body. Members do not discuss live cases in public or on social media.

Submit

Sign in to WHISTLE.

Both forms live inside the WHISTLE platform. Sign in with your Society credentials and you'll find the match report under "Today's appointments" and the incident form one tap away.
