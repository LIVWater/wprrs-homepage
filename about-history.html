Three decades of firsts.

Born from the unification of seven societies in 1992 — the first non-racial referees' body in South African rugby, and the nursery of officials who have shaped the modern game.

Western Province — one of the world's rugby powerhouses — is also the nursery of some of the top match officials, and has recorded several South African firsts.

When the country reorganised its rugby in 1992, the Province went first. Seven separate societies became one — a non-racial body, the earliest of its kind in South African rugby. The decisions made that year shaped not only the WPRRS, but the people who would go on to call international Tests for decades to come.

1992 — Unification

Seven societies, one union.

The amalgamation brought together seven historically separate organisations into the body that exists today.

The unification dissolved the racial boundaries that had previously divided the game's officials into parallel organisations. The contributing societies were:

  • Newlands-based WP Rugby Referees' Society
  • City and Suburban Referees
  • Tygerberg Rugby Referees
  • Somerset Board Referees
  • WP Rugby Football Board
  • Greenpoint-based WP Rugby Football Union
  • Members of the Boland Referees

An interim committee was tasked with carrying the new body through its first season. It was a small group, drawn from each of the founding societies, given the practical work of running fixtures while the constitution was drafted.

The interim committee

Fanie Wentzel, Farouk Williams, Dirk Jurgens, Neville Heilbron, Nico van Wyk, TC Hardenberg, Willem Brits, Gerald Njengele, Frank Greenblatt, Tinkie Heyns, Noor Karan, Douglas Holwill, James Apollis, Harry Abrahams, Paul Dobson, Sammy Loots, Chris Kuun, Salie Davids, Paul van Blommestein, and Dianne Roodman.

South African Firsts

The WPRRS honour roll.

Members of this Society have been first in a category that mattered, every time a barrier needed breaking. Each entry on this list opened a door for the next generation.

First

Black Currie Cup referee

Neville Heilbron — taking charge of South Africa's flagship domestic competition.

First

Black Test referee

Carl Spannenberg — opening the international door for South African officials of colour.

First

Female Test referee

Jenny Bentel — South Africa's first woman to officiate at international level.

First

Black African referee

Louis Mzomba — a foundational moment in the demographics of South African match officials.

First

Black international Match Assessor

James Apollis — moving from the whistle to the highest level of refereeing evaluation.

First

Modern-era WP referee in a Springbok Test

Marius van der Westhuizen — first member of the modern Society to take charge of an international involving South Africa.
