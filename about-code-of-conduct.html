Standards we hold ourselves to.

The Society adopts the SA Rugby Referees Association Code of Conduct — a single, consistent standard for behaviour on and off the field, in keeping with the values of the game.

Every active member of the WPRRS is bound by the SA Rugby Referees Association Code of Conduct. It applies on the field, in the changing room, on social media, and in any setting where a member is identified with the Society. The Code is built on six principles that translate the values of rugby into the specific responsibilities of a match official.

The principles

Six values, applied every match.

01
Integrity

Decisions are made honestly and applied consistently. A referee's word stands regardless of the score, the crowd, or the consequence.

02
Respect

Players, coaches, fellow officials, and opponents are treated with dignity. The whistle does not authorise contempt.

03
Impartiality

Personal interest, club affiliation, and previous encounters are set aside. The match is officiated on what happens, not who is involved.

04
Player welfare

Safety overrides every other consideration. BokSmart protocols, head-injury management, and contact-area policing are non-negotiable.

05
Professionalism

Preparation, presentation, punctuality, and post-match conduct all reflect on the Society. Every referee represents WPRRS in public.

06
Confidentiality

Match reviews, disciplinary discussions, and member matters remain inside the Society. Public commentary on individual referees is not the member's place.

In practice

What this means.

The principles translate into specific commitments that every member agrees to on joining and renews annually.

01

On the field

Apply the Laws as written, communicate clearly with players and assistants, manage the contact area with player welfare first, and protect the integrity of the result.

02

Off the field

Conduct yourself in clubhouses, at functions, and in public spaces in a way that reflects well on the Society. Decline gifts or hospitality that could compromise impartiality.

03

On social media

Do not publicly comment on individual matches, players, coaches, or fellow officials. Refrain from social media activity that brings the Society or rugby into disrepute.

04

Within the Society

Engage constructively with appointments, accept match reviews, follow the appeals procedure when contested, and support fellow members regardless of grade.

Discipline

When the Code is breached.

Breaches are dealt with under the Society's disciplinary procedure, which is set out in the By-Laws. The process is fair, confidential, and proportionate.

Reporting

A breach can be reported by any member, the executive, or a third party. The Clubhouse, Discipline & Liaison portfolio holder is the first point of contact.

Investigation

An investigation is opened, the member is informed, and they are given the opportunity to respond in writing or in person to the disciplinary committee.

Sanctions

Outcomes range from a written warning to suspension, demotion, or termination of membership. Sanctions are proportionate to the breach and consistent across cases.

Appeals

Any sanctioned member has the right to appeal to the executive. The appeals process is set out in the Constitution and By-Laws.
