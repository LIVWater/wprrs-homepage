Take up the whistle.

Aged 16 or older, male or female — if you love rugby and want to give back to the game, here is everything you need to start the journey.

The whistle is a different way to stay in the game — one that often opens more doors than playing ever did.

Refereeing keeps you on the field after your playing days end, lets you give back to a sport that gave you so much, and — for those with the drive — opens a pathway to provincial, national, and international honours that most players never reach.

Why become a referee

Five good reasons.

01
Stay in the game

When your playing career ends — by choice or injury — the whistle keeps you connected to the sport you love.

02
Give back

Every match needs an official. Rugby relies on volunteers and full-time refs to keep grassroots competition alive.

03
A second pathway

Many refs reach higher honours than they ever did as players — from Currie Cup to international Test rugby.

04
Be a leader

A ref runs 30 players' game. The decision-making, fitness, and presence build skills that transfer far beyond the pitch.

05
Earn while you ref

Graded referees receive match-day payments. Reach higher divisions and the financial return scales with you.

How to apply

Five steps to the first match.

The application process is structured so that by your first appointment you've completed safety certification, World Rugby online qualifications, and Level 1 training. Plan around three to six weeks.

01

Submit your application

Open the application form below, fill in your details, choose a membership tier, and pay the R250 registration fee in the same flow. The recruitment team confirms receipt and your Level 1 cohort within two working days.

  • R250 once-off registration
  • Pay by EFT, card, SnapScan or Zapper
  • Choose Standby or Active membership
Talk to the recruitment team
02

Complete the World Rugby online courses

Free, online, and worked through at your own pace. Create your World Rugby Passport account and complete all three modules.

  • Rugby Ready
  • Introduction to Rugby Refereeing
  • Laws of the Game
Open World Rugby Passport Law references
03

Send through your certificates

Download the electronic certificates from your World Rugby Passport and forward copies to the recruitment team. Three addresses ensure none get missed.

  • recruitmentrefs@wprugby.co.za
  • fsaid@wprugby.co.za
  • btheron@wprugby.co.za
Email certificates Office contact
04

Complete BokSmart certification

BokSmart is non-negotiable — every coach, referee and player support official must hold a valid BokSmart accreditation to enter the playing enclosure. We run accreditation courses several times a year.

Start MyBokSmart Upcoming WPRRS courses
05

World Rugby Level 1 with WPRRS

Once your online courses are complete, you'll join a Level 1 cohort run by the Society. This is where you bridge theory to the field — practical scenarios, signals, positioning, and a real test in match conditions.

Next Level 1 cohort Training pathway
After Level 1

What happens next.

Qualification is the beginning, not the end. The grading system rewards consistent on-field performance, fitness, and law knowledge — and the pathway leads directly to provincial and national appointments for those who put in the work.

The pathway

From first match to international ref.

01

Active or Standby

Choose your starting tier. Standby refs are appointed to their own club's home games. Active members are graded and appointed across the Province based on fitness and exam marks.

Membership
02

Regional coaching

Each region has a dedicated coach who runs sessions, reviews your matches, and gives feedback. This is where the on-field craft actually develops.

Find your regional coach → Development
03

Grade up

Three measurable elements determine promotion: fitness test results, examination marks, and on-field performance reviews. Hit the criteria, you move up a division.

Grading system → Grading
04

Provincial & national

From Division 1 the path opens to the SARRA National Panel and national appointments. Western Province has produced Currie Cup, Test, and international referees — the pathway is real.

National
Eligibility & fees

The simple requirements.

Who can apply

Anyone aged 16 or older. We actively recruit female and male applicants from across Western Province — no playing experience required.

Registration fee

R250 once-off on application. Banking details are included on the application form. Proof of payment must accompany your submission.

Two membership tiers

Standby Referees — appointed to home games at your own club.
Active Referees — graded and appointed across the Province by fitness and exam marks.
