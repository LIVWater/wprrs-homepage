01 · Match day

Prepare like a professional.

The protocol runs from confirming the appointment on Monday to filing the report on Saturday evening. Same arc, every level.

Full match-day guide
01

Confirm & review

Acknowledge the appointment in WHISTLE. Confirm kick-off time, venue, competition and any specific protocols. Refresh current World Rugby Laws and local regulations for the level.

Pre-match
02

Equipment & arrival

Whistle plus two spares, watch, cards, notebook, kit and studs. Arrive 60 minutes before kick-off — earlier for festivals or unfamiliar venues — and walk the field for hazards.

Pre-match
03

Briefing & in-game

Meet both captains 15 minutes before kick-off — scrum engagement, tackle area, foul play, and the BokSmart Blue Card procedure. Manage the contest with consistent communication and clear signals.

In-match
04

Final whistle & report

Confirm the score with both captains, thank the touch judges, and file the match report through WHISTLE before close of business. Serious incidents file separately, same day.

Post-match
02 · Reporting

One per match — and one when it matters.

Every appointed match generates a report. Every serious incident generates a dedicated incident report. Both submit through WHISTLE on the same day.

01

Match Report

Fixture details, half-time and full-time scores, cards, injuries, and match notes. Pre-populated for each appointment in WHISTLE; submit by 18:00 on match day.

02

Incident Report

Filed for foul play sent off, abuse of officials, BokSmart Blue Card incidents, citings, or any matter likely to require a disciplinary hearing. Routes directly to the discipline committee.
